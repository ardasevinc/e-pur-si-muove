Galileo Galilei

Galileo Galilei. An Italian philosopher, astronomer and mathematician born in 1564 changed the world of physical sciences. He contributed to astronomy, physics, materials science and many more fields.


He defended the heliocentric view of our solar system where the earth wasn't the center that everything was revolving around. Earth was revolving around the sun.


His findings did not do him any good while he was alive, though. He faced the catholic church and inquisition, spending his last 8 years in house arrest.


He is believed to be said "Eppur si muove" (And yet it moves). The portrait of him above portrays Galileo holding a nail in hand, looking at the words "E pur si muove"
